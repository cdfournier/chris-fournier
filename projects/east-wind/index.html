Freelance projectEast Wind

Logo design, branding, merchandising strategies for an exclusive private property on the island of Antigua.

Introduction

East Wind is a five-bedroom luxury retreat located on Jumby Bay Island in Antigua. I was asked to design a logo and create branding and merchandising concepts. The client had few stipulations: the work had to evoke an Asian influence, it had to appeal to both family and friends, as well as the invested guests, and it also had to feature the color purple.

Project details

  • Brief:Create a brand identity with an Asian influence using the color purple for an exclusive private property on the island of Antigua that could be easily utilized across all branding applications without modification
  • Audience:High net-worth individuals looking for a luxury property to lease in Antigua, as well as the welcome guests and visitors of the property

Designing the logo

The client had an idea in his head and was keen to develop the logo quickly. I used an iterative approach to build a cadence we could adjust to later. We looked at existing logos they liked and did not like, we looked at icons and emblems, fonts and typography options, and much more.

These iterative sessions with the client helped the logo to take shape. We would choose a font, and then we would set about fine-tuning the character shapes to make the text unique. The same thing occurred with the iconography: we fell in love with a particular tree treatment, but there were sessions over branches, the horizon, where should the word marks would goâ€¦

Application of the logo

While working with the client on the design of the logo, I was also hard at work on the strategy for it’s use. We knew there would be required items like signage, clothing, and linens. I also mocked up other items as part of the merchandising strategy: a trendy retro turntable, stationery kits including notebooks, pens, pencils, and a rubber stamp. Branded bottled water and toiletries would add an executive level ambiance for all guests.
