Introduction to Panera Bread

Case StudyPanera Bread

Panera Bread is an American chain of fast casual restaurants in the United States and Canada.

Panera Bread Project Details

Deliverables

User Experience Strategy, Design, and HTML/CSS Prototypes

Background

In 2016 I began to study Panera’s digital landscape, looking for ways to capitalize on its strengths as a brand and deliver an elevated digital experience to users. I recognized that the current digital ecosystem was flawed, and there were missed opportunities to connect with users and drive e-commerce.

I led the research, strategy, design, and build of a functional prototype to showcase a new digital user experience design for the brand, which resulted in a 300% increase in funding year-over-year for Panera’s web projects.

Brief

Create a rendering to socialize and test a new strategy and new design for Panera Bread’s digital ecosystem.

Audience

Panera’s leadership team, internal stakeholders, and external partners.

Strategy

Highlight key strategic pillars to improve the site UX, drive engagement, brand messaging, and e-commerce across the user journey.

Intended Outcomes

Internal alignment on and approval of site strategies, tactics and concepts from key stakeholders to obtain funding for a site overhaul that addressed the entire customer journey through the marketing funnel, as well as support other business channels such as catering and gift cards.

The aim externally was to grow awareness, acquisition, retention, and loyalty through an enhanced user experience designed to exceed key objectives and performance indicators.

My Process

I conducted a series of influencer interviews and combed through the data and analytics. This research showed clearly that there were three things that mattered most to users:

Moments That Matter

  1. Menu exploration
  2. Rewards
  3. Commerce

Based on these key learnings, I envisioned a new user experience strategy designed to:

Business Objectives

  1. Increase transaction referrals (and revenue) from the brand site
  2. Deepen brand engagement (recency, frequency, depth, and duration)
  3. Foster a better understanding of user behavior to enable iteration and optimization

I designed the first fully functional, responsive prototype for this new strategy and user experience in late 2016. I used actual live menu data in an effort to socialize the strategy with Panera’s leadership team and demonstrate key pillars of a new user experience:

Key Tactics

  1. A modern, mobile-first responsive design
  2. Heightened transparency and priority given to rewards for known users
  3. Convenient and prominent paths to commerce including personalized options based on prior behavior
  4. Simplified navigation (based on the moments that matter) and content supporting Panera’s brand values woven directly into the menu-browsing experience
  5. Promotion of other business channels, like gift cards and catering, as part of the content strategy

I built modular components that could be reused throughout the site and by using a singular image size ratio to enable greater fidelity. Then, as the site came together it was easy and efficient to go back and fine-tune each component.

The prototypes I developed were proofs of a concept designed to highlight the benefits of responsive design to key stakeholders, such as increased speed and development process, efficiency, and a reduced technical code debt.

In 2017 I provided guidance to design partner Work & Co. for a new omni-channel digital design. As the visual design concept evolved, I was able to incorporate their designs into an updated responsive prototype that we could test in the browser, while maintaining the key strategic pillars I had outlined. The browser testing was intended to shorten the time from design to development and launch, as well as catch and correct issues before they became a problem.

Check out the final responsive prototype I built as part of the project.

Results

The prototype was successful in achieving buy-in from senior leadership. My work resulted in a 300% increase in funding year-over-year for Panera’s web projects.
