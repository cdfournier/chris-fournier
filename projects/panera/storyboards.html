Design goalsKeep it clean

Everything came together in the user interface and experience: Moments That Matter, business objectives, key tactics, design, and content strategy. In order to be successful, however, it was crucial to focus and make hard decisions.

Scroll down to learn more about my clean design plan.

The year was 2017

The interface lacked clear calls-to-action and used space poorly.

Key tacticA modern, mobile-first responsive design

Key tacticHeightened transparency and priority given to rewards for known users

Key tacticConvenient and prominent paths to commerce

Utilize analytics and personalization to customize the experience with suggestions.

Key tacticSimplified navigation based on the Moments That Matter

Don’t make me think

The existing interfaces suffered from too many calls-to-action.

Key tacticConvenient and prominent paths to commerce

Six menu categories at the top and a single “Order Now” button for each product.

Key tacticContent supporting Panera’s brand values woven directly into the menu-browsing experience

Like defining “clean ingredients”.

By placing content and commerce side-by-side you enable users who want to quickly make a purchase, as well as users who want to do some exploration and connect with the brand.
