Introduction to MyStuff

Functional PrototypesMyStuff

A prototype for a new service designed to make it wicked easy to catalog the important details of all of your stuff.

MyStuff project details

Deliverables

UX design, HTML, CSS, and jQuery

I enjoy building my own browser-based prototypes to test user experiences. This is a way to constantly learn and iterate, and inform and refine my design approach with direct user feedback.

Brief

I built a personal inventory app — a tool that would enable users to create a catalog of their valuables in case of catastrophe. The goal of the app was to enable users to easily catalog and add their items.

We take for granted that we are usually safe from disaster and property loss. Should a disaster strike, it would be helpful to have a record of your property.

Audience

Everyone with a vested interest in holding records of personal value: home owners, insurance companies, small business, and others.

My Process

I used Sketch to create user journey maps so that I could fully visualize an end-to-end experience. I broke the flow into screens and decision-points. This enabled me to design something that would be simple and intuitive to use without the need for elaborate instructions.

I typically don’t use frameworks, but I do employ some techniques that enable me to get a jump on building quickly. With this app, I was having some trouble putting the screens together the way I envisioned them, so I jumped back into Sketch to create a component library.

Outcome

The prototype enabled me to practice my problem solving skills and refine my build approach, while also getting to test new UX designs.
