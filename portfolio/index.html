Work Maxims

Defining Success

Every project must be rigorously driven and stringently evaluated by the same criteria: the Brand's Mission, Vision, Values, and Goals.

Gatekeeping

Don't do anything unless it's great. If it's a good idea, work on it until it's great. If it's not even good, then forget about it and move on.

Keep It Simple

The easier the better. Complicated ideas and messages run the risk of getting lost in today's fast-paced world of information overload.

Don't Pile On

Often really great ideas become overburdened by the need to incorporate "everyone's ideas". Resist that urge.

Don't Chip Away

The opposite of the pile-on. Don't let people chip away at a great idea, reducing it to a lesser version of itself.

Be Wary of Opinions

President John F. Kennedy said, "Too often we enjoy the comfort of opinion without the discomfort of thought."

Visualize Everything

Storyboard every customer touchpoint to make sure that the plan works well and stays on message from every angle. Every experience must be a coherent delivery of the brand's promise.

Wait Until It's Ready

When it comes to implementation, do not rush. If it is not ready, then do not launch. A launch delay is much better than launching a flawed campaign on-time.
